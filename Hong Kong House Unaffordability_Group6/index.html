Downturn Property Market, But Increasingly Unaffordable Housing in Hong Kong

Following the dip in Hong Kong private home prices to their lowest point since October 2016 in January, the domestic property price index in Hong Kong saw a slight decline in February despite the announcement made in February's Budget to abolish stamp duty.


Photo by Aleksandar Pasaric

Accompanying the regression to property price levels seen eight years ago is the decline in the real estate and related professional services industry's contribution to Hong Kong's GDP. This sector, which accounted for approximately 11% of the GDP in 2012, has dwindled to about 8% by 2022, according to government statistics. (See Page 1)

However, it does not necessarily mean that housing has become more affordable.

According to government statistics, compared to 2012, property prices have surged by 80%, whereas median household income has only increased by around 40%. The high property prices continue to deter the majority of the citizens. (See Page 2)

The World's Most Unaffordable Housing Prices

According to the 2023 Demographia International Housing Affordability report published by the Urban Reform Institute, the top three most unaffordable housing markets globally are Hong Kong, Sydney, and Vancouver. Hong Kong leads the list with a median multiple of 18.8, indicating that the median house price in Hong Kong is 18.8 times the median household income in 2022. As early as 2012, Hong Kong had already been ranked as the world's most unaffordable market by this institute , with its median multiple increasing by 39.26% over the past decade.

Housing Rents Reached New Heights Last Year

In contrast to the consecutive declines in property prices, Hong Kong's domestic rent index has been steadily rising for 11 months since January 2023, only beginning to decline again in January this year, as reported by the Rating and Valiation Department. (See Line Shart Below)

There were 10 out of 18 districts in Hong Kong where rent consumed more than 30% of a household's monthly income last year.

Among all, the highest ratio in 2022 was found in Kwun Tong District, where a median-income household renting there would have spent nearly 43.8% of their income on an average-priced apartment.

In addition to the lowest ratio found in Sai Kung District, where a median-income household renting there would have spent 21.61% of their income on an average-priced apartment, the other seven districts had median-income households renting higher than 25% last year.

Worse Burden for Hong Kong's Youth

Although public housing can be used as an alternative to purchasing private residential flats, there are still constraints, such as a long waiting time and a limited quota.

According to the latest data released by the Hong Kong government, the average waiting time for public housing is 5.6 years, and single and young adults normally have to wait longer to apply for public housing than those living with children or older family members.

According to Hong Kong Youth Housing Aspirations 2023 Survey Analysis Report, when asked how young people like the government to help with their housing issues, 79.8% of the respondents stated that they would like to be provided interest-free loans for their first house purchase, which is the solution they like most.

According to thelatest statistics released by the Census and Statistics Department , the median income for first-time university graduates in Hong Kong in 2022 is HK$17,424 per month, while the median monthly income for the average Hong Kong citizen is HK$20,000. It means that if those young graduates want to rent a single-person apartment in Kowloon District, where it is convenient for commuting, they have to spend at least 33% of their monthly income. It can be a huge burden to the fresh graduate.
